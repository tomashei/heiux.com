Xinhee Design Center

it as a building with skin-and-bones

My Role

Parametric Designer

Location

Xiamen, Fujian, China

Design Area

60 000 m²

Team

Ma Yansong
Dang Qun
Yosuke Hayano

Situation:


  • A draft idea of the membrane facade had been accepted by the client.
  • The client was worried about the costs of the project realization.
  • Construction costs could not be easily estimated.
  • The leading architect wanted to prepare a wide spectrum of pattern conceptions.

A bird-view presenting building and its innovative facade.

Task:


  • to find a design solution compromising: load capabilities and construction costs;
  • to ensure the client that the proposed conception could be realized;
  • to keep the shape of the rationalized facade as close to the initial surfaces as possible;
  • to present as many variations of facade pasterns as possible in limited time;

A view into bone structure of the building.

Action:


  • Liaison between the consultant and the client to make sure they both accepted the design.
  • Code a Grasshopper script that would optimize the membrane facade and be ﬂexible to quickly changing the design.
  • Develop multiple design options to be presented to the client.
  • Send a design package to the client and the consultant in order to explain the design, levels of rationalization, and proposed facade patterns.
  • Use genetic algorithms to optimize the accepted design option.

A complex script used for optimisation of the facade.

Result:


  • The final version of the pattern design was approved by the leading architect.
  • Overall construction costs were estimated.
  • The client was satisfied and accepted the design.
  • Rationalized geometry was sent to the consultant for further development.

A perspective from inside the building, presenting membrane-like effect of the facade.
