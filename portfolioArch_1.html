Harbin Opera House

a cultural center of the future

My Role

Parametric Designer

Location

Harbin, Heilongjiang, China

Design Area

77 000 m²

Team

Ma Yansong
Yosuke Hayano
Dang Qun



“We envision Harbin Opera House as a cultural center of the future – a tremendous performance venue, as well as a dramatic public space that embodies the integration of human, art and the city identity, while synergistically blending with the surrounding nature.”

Ma Yansong

Situation:


  • The construction of the building was already in progress.
  • All the materials had been delivered to the construction site.
  • A team responsible for the construction of a wood-shell in the main hall announced inconsistencies in the project - they were not able to continue without more details.
  • The client and the construction company were getting worried about the opening date.

Image © Adam Mork

Task:


  • to understand the problem, and to see what could be done not to delay the deadline;
  • to ensure the client that opening date would not have to be moved;
  • to deliver a correct design solution as soon as possible;

Image © Adam Mork

Action:


  • Negotiate the rearrangement of the schedule with the construction company in order to obtain a few additional days to find a solution to the problem.
  • Analyze existing project documentation and find out how the issue could be solved in the simplest and most effective way.
  • Prepare a parametric program in Grasshopper that would allow for quick visualization of design options, and could be used on all elements containing issues.
  • Deliver corrected plans to the construction company as soon as possible.

Image © Adam Mork

Result:


  • The construction schedule was not interrupted, and the building was opened in time.
